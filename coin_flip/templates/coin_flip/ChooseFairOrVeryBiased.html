The experiment proceeds in rounds, with each round having three subrounds. In each subround, two coins will be flipped. You will not be informed of their outcomes automatically.

Each subround has two questions. First, you will be given the option to find out the outcome of one of the two coins. The first question will ask you which coin you'd like to reveal. Once you choose the coin, the outcome will be revealed to you on the next page. After that, you will be asked to guess the outcome of both coins.

You are now asked to choose to reveal the outcome of either the {{ coins.0.1 }} coin or the {{ coins.1.1 }} coin.

The {{ coins.0.1 }} coin has a probability of 0.5 heads, while the {{ coins.1.1 }} coin has a 0.95 probability of heads.

The {{ coins.0.1 }} coin is worth $1, and the {{ coins.1.1 }} coin is worth $1. If you guess correctly, you will receive the sum of $2. If not, you will receive $0. You will not, however, see whether you won immediately. Your winnings will be revealed only at the end of the 10 rounds.

This process will continue for 10 rounds. The experiment will end after you complete all 10 rounds.
